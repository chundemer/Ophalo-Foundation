# Keep Founder Checklist

**Purpose:** Personal checklist for Christian while backend sessions continue.
**Goal:** Turn Keep from a strong backend/product idea into a pilot-ready business.

## Product Compass

- [ ] Define the pilot promise in one sentence.
  - **Reason:** Every feature, screen, and pilot conversation needs one shared test.
  - **Done when:** You can say the promise from memory in plain language, such as: "Every customer request gets captured, acknowledged, owned, and followed up until the loop is closed."

- [ ] Write the "Keep does not manage the work. Keep manages the promise." boundary into your own words.
  - **Reason:** This protects the product from becoming ServiceTitan-lite.
  - **Done when:** You can quickly decide whether a new idea belongs in Keep, belongs later, or belongs outside the product.

- [ ] List what Keep will not do in v1.
  - **Reason:** Clear exclusions reduce scope drift.
  - **Done when:** Scheduling, invoices, payments, inventory, payroll, route optimization, and full CRM/marketing are explicitly out of v1 unless a later decision changes that.

## Pilot Businesses

- [ ] Create a 30-day pilot plan for the HVAC business.
  - **Reason:** HVAC tests team workflow: admins, operators, assignment, unassigned work, response speed, closeout, and feedback review.
  - **Done when:** You know who the weekly champion is, who the admins/operators are, how requests will enter Keep, and which metrics you will review weekly.

- [ ] Create a 30-day pilot plan for the two-person contractor.
  - **Reason:** This pilot tests simplicity, mobile-first workflow, slow-job communication, customer confidence, and tiny-team UI.
  - **Done when:** You know how they will use intake links, manual entry, customer updates, completion, and closeout without feeling burdened.

- [ ] Decide the free pilot terms.
  - **Reason:** Free pilots should be a serious exchange, not an indefinite favor.
  - **Done when:** You have a written expectation: 60-90 days free, weekly feedback, real use, permission to use anonymized learnings, and an early-adopter rate after the pilot.

- [ ] Identify the weekly champion for each pilot.
  - **Reason:** Without one accountable person, feedback gets vague and usage fades.
  - **Done when:** You know exactly who will meet with you weekly and tell you what worked, what failed, and what confused the team.

## Pilot Workflow Notes

- [ ] Write the HVAC "day in the life" workflow.
  - **Reason:** This exposes missing product steps before UI work starts.
  - **Done when:** The flow covers: customer request arrives, admin sees it, operator receives/claims it, customer gets update, contact is logged, operator marks completed, Owner/Admin closes/reviews.

- [ ] Write the contractor "day in the life" workflow.
  - **Reason:** The two-person workflow should feel lightweight, not like ticket software.
  - **Done when:** The flow covers: customer submits request, contractor gets alerted, customer gets personal page, contractor sends updates during slow work, request is completed/closed.

- [ ] Write the customer journey.
  - **Reason:** Keep wins when the customer feels acknowledged and confident.
  - **Done when:** You can describe what the customer sees from intake to personal page to updates to feedback.

- [ ] Define the manual-entry bridge.
  - **Reason:** Businesses may start by entering requests manually, but the long-term behavior is sharing the intake link.
  - **Done when:** You have a simple explanation: manual entry is allowed, but the intake link reduces calls, phone tag, and admin data entry.

## Screen Inventory

- [ ] Draft the web Owner/Admin screen inventory.
  - **Reason:** Web is the command center for setup, control, review, and history.
  - **Done when:** You have a list covering setup, team invites, intake link, request list, detail, assignment oversight, feedback review, closeout, search/history, settings.

- [ ] Draft the native operator screen inventory.
  - **Reason:** Native should stay narrow and fast.
  - **Done when:** You have a list covering push alert, my work, a prominent Available/Unassigned view, request detail, call customer, log contact, send update, mark completed, watch/mute.

- [ ] Draft the customer page screen inventory.
  - **Reason:** The customer page is the trust moment.
  - **Done when:** You know what the customer can see and do without exposing internal details.

- [ ] Identify tiny-team UI simplification needs.
  - **Reason:** One- and two-person businesses should not see heavyweight team-routing controls they do not need.
  - **Done when:** You have notes for solo, two-person, and 3+ team surfaces without changing backend visibility rules.

## Measurement And Impact

- [ ] Define the first weekly pilot impact summary.
  - **Reason:** You need evidence that Keep is useful before charging.
  - **Done when:** The summary includes requests captured, customer-created requests, median first response time, response-within-target rate, stale/overdue follow-ups, customer updates, external contacts, unresolved feedback, and closeout backlog.

- [ ] Decide which metrics are customer-facing vs internal-only.
  - **Reason:** Business impact reporting and founder/admin observability are different products.
  - **Done when:** Customer-facing metrics prove value to the business; internal metrics help you support pilots and detect adoption problems.

- [ ] Write the honest revenue-impact language.
  - **Reason:** Keep should not overclaim revenue attribution.
  - **Done when:** Your wording says Keep helps protect revenue by surfacing requests at risk of being forgotten, delayed, or left without clear follow-up.

- [ ] Decide which optional business-outcome fields can wait.
  - **Reason:** Estimated job value, won/lost, referral source, and repeat customer data may help later but can slow v1.
  - **Done when:** You know what is deferred until after pilot learning.

## Pricing And Packaging

- [ ] Keep the pricing hypothesis visible.
  - **Reason:** Pricing should be informed by pilot usage, not locked too early.
  - **Done when:** You can compare real pilot value against $49-$79, $99, $199, and $299+ price points.

- [ ] Track pricing inputs during pilots.
  - **Reason:** Team size, operator count, request volume, notification usage, and impact metrics will shape packaging.
  - **Done when:** You have a place to record active users, active operators, requests/month, customer-created requests, and perceived value.

- [ ] Decide early-adopter terms.
  - **Reason:** Free pilots need a next step.
  - **Done when:** You know whether pilot businesses get a discounted 12-month rate, what rate it is, and what feedback/testimonial/referral you expect.

## Internal Admin And Founder Visibility

- [ ] Define the internal admin dashboard needs.
  - **Reason:** The reference app felt blind; you need visibility into onboarding and usage.
  - **Done when:** You have a list covering new business onboarded, owner invited team, intake link created/shared, first request received, active users, feature usage, inactive accounts, worker/notification failures.

- [ ] Define high-signal internal alerts.
  - **Reason:** You want real-time awareness without notification noise.
  - **Done when:** You know which events deserve mobile alerts: new business onboarded, stalled onboarding, pilot account went inactive, delivery/work failure, unusual usage drop.

- [ ] Define internal access safety rules.
  - **Reason:** Sysadmin visibility can become risky if not bounded.
  - **Done when:** You have rules: internal access is permission-gated, read-only first, audited, metadata-light alerts, no broad impersonation by default.

## Go-To-Market Notes

- [ ] Write the "we already have software" answer.
  - **Reason:** This will be the most common objection.
  - **Done when:** You can say: "Keep works beside your existing tools. It protects the customer follow-up loop your current tools still let fall into texts, voicemails, memory, and phone tag."

- [ ] Write the "why pay for better communication?" answer.
  - **Reason:** Small businesses need to connect communication to trust, referrals, repeat work, and fewer lost opportunities.
  - **Done when:** You can explain how faster response, visible ownership, customer pages, and unresolved feedback review protect reputation and future revenue.

- [ ] Write the first landing-page promise.
  - **Reason:** Future marketing should be simple before it is clever.
  - **Done when:** You have 3-5 headline/copy options based on the product compass.

- [ ] Collect pilot story examples.
  - **Reason:** Real stories will sell better than feature lists.
  - **Done when:** During pilots, you capture examples of a request caught, a customer reassured, duplicate work avoided, or unresolved feedback reviewed.

## Build Coordination

- [ ] Declare the pilot product scope locked.
  - **Reason:** The core Keep loop is now defined enough to shift from product architecture into development, deployment, QA, onboarding, and implementation.
  - **Done when:** New feature ideas are accepted into docs/deferred topics, but do not enter go-live scope unless they are security/privacy/compliance issues, data-model corrections, or true pilot blockers.

- [ ] Review Session 4 implementation against pilot workflows.
  - **Reason:** Filters/search/history/pagination should support real admin/operator work.
  - **Done when:** You can map each Session 4 view to a pilot need.

- [ ] Review Session 5 implementation against feedback-review workflow.
  - **Reason:** Feedback review is part of proving the loop was closed.
  - **Done when:** Mark reviewed, review notes, and queue navigation feel clear for Owner/Admin.

- [ ] Prepare Session 6 questions before the decision doc.
  - **Reason:** Ready-to-close and stale status check affect pilot closeout behavior.
  - **Done when:** You know what counts as stale, who sees it, and how Owner/Admin should close work efficiently.

- [ ] Plan the V1 notification/device slice without pulling in the full notification platform.
  - **Reason:** Basic native push and badges are core to Keep's accountability loop, but custom preferences, quiet hours, retries/outbox, delivery analytics, and channel matrices are still later.
  - **Done when:** You have a bounded pre-go-live plan for device registration, server-derived badge counts, minimal APNs/FCM push/deep links, actor exclusion, participation/mute/OffSeason suppression, and fail-soft delivery.

- [ ] Keep the V1 freshness matrix explicit in web/native planning.
  - **Reason:** V1 should feel alive without adding SSE/WebSockets too early.
  - **Done when:** UI plans include refetch-after-write, focus/resume sync, pull-to-refresh, 30-60 second active-list polling, server-derived counts/badges, and push/deep links for urgent off-screen work.

- [ ] Build the in-app Report Friction pilot hook.
  - **Reason:** Pilot users are busy running their businesses; if they have to leave Keep to report a bug, confusing moment, or missing need, most feedback will disappear.
  - **Done when:** Authenticated pilot users can tap Report Friction, type a short note, optionally choose a category, and send compact app/account/screen context through the OpHalo API to your private founder/internal channel without exposing webhook secrets or customer PII.

- [ ] Build the in-app Pilot Updates page.
  - **Reason:** Pilot teams need to know known issues, workarounds, and what changed without searching email, texts, or release notes.
  - **Done when:** The app has a simple Help/Pilot Updates page with Known Issues, What's New, Coming Soon, last-updated timestamp, and a direct Report Friction entrypoint.

## Pilot Readiness Edge Cases

- [ ] Review SMS/text consent language before go-live.
  - **Reason:** If Keep or the business later sends text updates, customer phone capture should not create compliance ambiguity. This needs legal review before production SMS.
  - **Done when:** Intake/customer forms have approved consent/disclosure language or a deliberate decision that v1 does not send platform SMS.

- [ ] Confirm request-level address/location behavior.
  - **Reason:** A repeat customer may request work at a new home, rental, or job site. Customer identity reuse must not overwrite useful historical location context.
  - **Done when:** V1 captures service location as request-level data, and any customer-level address update/merge behavior is deliberately deferred or decided.

- [ ] Define how garbage/spam/test public-intake requests are handled.
  - **Reason:** Public intake can create nonsense requests that clutter the command center and distort response metrics.
  - **Done when:** Required fields and validation are clear, Admin can cancel garbage requests cleanly, and impact metrics can exclude cancelled/spam/test requests where appropriate.

- [ ] Write the operator app-switching orientation.
  - **Reason:** Field workers may need Keep plus Jobber/ServiceTitan/other tools. If Keep feels like duplicate work, operators will ignore it.
  - **Done when:** Pilot training frames Keep as the customer-contact fast lane: call, update, log contact, mark completed; execution details stay in the field-service tool.

- [ ] Decide how operators will notice available/unassigned work.
  - **Reason:** If an operator's default list is empty, they may assume there is no work unless the Available queue is obvious.
  - **Done when:** Native/web operator layouts show available work as a top-level tab, toggle, or count rather than hiding it inside advanced filters.

- [ ] Define the ready-to-close customer-activity warning.
  - **Reason:** Admins should not close a request without noticing that the customer replied or gave negative feedback after the technician left.
  - **Done when:** Session 6 ready-to-close design includes a clear indicator for customer activity, unresolved attention, or feedback since completion.

- [ ] Decide how post-close customer follow-up is logged for unresolved feedback.
  - **Reason:** If an Owner/Admin calls a customer after negative feedback, using only an internal note may feel inconsistent with the normal contact-log workflow.
  - **Done when:** You decide whether Closed requests with active unresolved-feedback attention can accept structured external-contact logs, and what those logs do not do: reopen, change status, count first response, notify the customer, or automatically mark feedback reviewed.

- [ ] Preserve the Snooze / Waiting Until idea without pulling it into go-live by default.
  - **Reason:** Parts delays, booked-out contractors, weather, and third-party waits are real. They should eventually pause stale alerts without changing lifecycle status.
  - **Done when:** Deferred topic `DEF-067` remains visible, and the candidate direction is an internal action that sets `NextAttentionAtUtc` with a reason/note.

- [ ] Preserve quick replies with a promise-safety rule.
  - **Reason:** Canned responses help field operators move fast, but "we will get back to you" becomes harmful if it clears attention without a follow-up reminder.
  - **Done when:** Deferred topic `DEF-068` captures informational vs promise-bearing templates, and promise-bearing replies require or prompt a reminder.

- [ ] Keep attention actions obvious for field users.
  - **Reason:** When an alert fires, the operator should immediately know what to do and whether that action clears the alert.
  - **Done when:** List/detail UI uses backend action metadata to show clear primary actions and effect summaries: customer-visible/internal, clears attention, counts first response, and status unchanged/changed.

- [ ] Revisit the end-of-day open-promises summary after core flows are stable.
  - **Reason:** "0 customers waiting on us" is a strong Owner/Admin habit, but it should derive from the command center rather than become a new reporting project too early.
  - **Done when:** Deferred topic `DEF-071` is reviewed after list/detail/closeout work, using real pilot behavior.
